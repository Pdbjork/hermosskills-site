Privacy Policy

Effective: June 29, 2026 · hermosskills · A Peter Bjork project

The short version: We need your email and payment confirmation to run sponsorships and commissions. We do not see or store the prompts you run against any sponsored skill — that runs on your own infrastructure. We do not sell or share your data. You can ask us to delete everything we have on you at any time, and we will within 30 days.

2. What we never see

This is the section most people skim, so it gets its own:

  • We do not see the prompts you run against any skill in the catalog.
  • We do not see the outputs the skills produce for you.
  • We do not see API keys, model endpoints, or other infrastructure details of the agents you operate.
  • We do not read the contents of repositories, datasets, or documents you load into a skill.

hermosskills is a marketplace and editorial layer. The agent workflows where skills are used run on your own infrastructure, not on our servers. If you ever feel we have crossed that line, contact us immediately.

3. How we use what we collect

We use the data listed in section 1 to:

  • Process sponsorship and commission payments.
  • Route maintainer payouts and the 30% public-good rebate.
  • Send transactional emails: receipts, renewal notices, tax documents, editorial briefings.
  • Publish the optional Founding Sponsor credit on skill pages.
  • Comply with US tax and business law (we keep payment records for 7 years, as required by the IRS).

We do not use your data for advertising. We do not use your data to train any AI model. We do not sell or "rent" your data to anyone, ever.

We will also share data if a US court orders us to, but we will challenge overly broad requests and notify you if legally permitted.

5. Cookies and analytics

The hermosskills site uses only strictly necessary cookies and local storage (for things like "you have dismissed the funding banner"). We do not use Google Analytics, Meta Pixel, or any third-party advertising trackers.

If we add analytics in the future, we will update this policy and ask for consent where required by your state's law.

6. Your rights under US state privacy laws

If you live in California, Virginia, Colorado, Connecticut, Utah, Texas, Oregon, Montana, Delaware, Iowa, New Hampshire, New Jersey, Tennessee, Minnesota, Maryland, Indiana, Kentucky, Rhode Island, or another state with a comprehensive privacy law, you have the right to:

  • Know what personal data we have about you.
  • Delete it, subject to the exceptions in section 7.
  • Correct inaccurate data.
  • Port your data in a machine-readable format.
  • Opt out of sale or sharing — we don't sell or share, so this is automatic.
  • Non-discrimination for exercising your rights.

To exercise these rights, email team@hermosskills.com from the address on file. We will respond within 45 days.

California "Shine the Light" (Civil Code § 1798.83): we do not share your data with third parties for their direct marketing purposes.

When the retention period ends, we delete the data within 30 days. If you ask us to delete sooner, we will — except for records we are legally required to keep (tax records), which we isolate from anything that can be used to contact you.

8. How we protect it

We use industry-standard safeguards: TLS for all data in transit, encrypted storage at rest, and access limited to the project owner (Pete) plus a single, password-managed Cloudflare account. Stripe is PCI-DSS Level 1 certified.

No system is 100% secure. If we ever discover a breach affecting your data, we will notify you within 72 hours by email and post a notice on this page.

9. Children's privacy

hermosskills is intended for adult developers, founders, and operators. We do not knowingly collect data from anyone under 16. If we learn we have, we will delete it within 30 days.

10. Changes to this policy

If we change this policy in a material way, we will update the "Effective" date, post a banner on the home page, and email active sponsors. Non-material changes (typos, clarifications) will be made silently.

This is a plain-language privacy policy. It is not legal advice. If you have specific legal questions, consult a lawyer licensed in your state.
